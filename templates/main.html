Pragman Family Farms

Healthy Food, Grown with High-Tech Infrastructure in Alaska

Our Vision

At Pragman Family Farms, our vision is rooted in the principles of radical self-sufficiency and a solar-punk ethos. We believe in empowering communities—especially remote ones—by leveraging cutting-edge technologies and ecologically conscious practices to produce nutritious food year-round in Alaska.

We are driven by a spirit of innovation and experimentation, working in our own backyard garage to refine hydroponic systems, 3D-printed components, and sustainable energy approaches. Our hope is to demonstrate that small-scale, high-tech agriculture can thrive in the harshest environments, forging a path for local food independence and resilience.

Technology & Infrastructure

We proudly utilize Bambu Labs X1C 3D printers to prototype and refine our hydroponic systems, basing our designs on open-source solutions provided by generous makers around the world. From custom grow baskets (crafted using OpenSCAD) to modular vertical farms, we blend DIY ethos with industry-grade engineering to overcome the unique challenges of Alaskan agriculture.

Our current systems are designed to be replicated, scaled, and adapted in remote communities, focusing on minimal waste, energy efficiency, and ease of maintenance. Ultimately, our goal is to provide “Vegetables as a Service” worldwide—one layer of 3D-printed hardware at a time.

About Us

Pragman Family Farms began as a passion project in a small Alaskan garage. Led by a team of makers, problem-solvers, and dreamers, we see each prototype and harvest as a step toward a more self-sufficient future.

Whether we’re testing new growth substrates or refining automation scripts, our spirit remains the same: to challenge traditional notions of food production and pave the way for resilient, technology-driven agriculture in the world’s most challenging climates.
